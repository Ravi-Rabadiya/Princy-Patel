About me

Hello, and welcome to my creative world! My name is Princy Patel, a 20-year-old artist from India with a deep passion for transforming imagination into reality through my art. While my artistic journey is a significant part of who I am, I am also pursuing a bachelor’s degree in nursing, driven by a heartfelt ambition to make a difference in the lives of others.

My goal

My ultimate goal is to become a doctor, with a mission to provide free medical care to those in need, ensuring that poverty is never a barrier to receiving treatment. This vision fuels my dedication to both my studies and my artistic pursuits.

My Interests

Exploring new places and cultures to find inspiration and broaden my perspective.

Capturing the beauty of the world through my lens, preserving moments that tell unique stories.

Bringing ideas to life with pencil and paper, expressing emotions and concepts through intricate designs.

More about me

Art for me is not just a hobby—it’s a way of communicating, inspiring, and connecting with others. Alongside my creative endeavors, I am committed to a future of compassion and service, blending my skills in art and healthcare to create a meaningful impact.

Thank you for stopping by and learning a little about me. I hope my story inspires you to pursue your own passions and dreams!

Reflection

Reflection perfectly describes my journey over the past sixteen weeks. I have learned so much in such a short period of time. Looking back at my first attempt at writing, I struggled to even get started. My thoughts were scattered, and I didn’t know how to organize them on paper. However, I gave it my best effort and was able to improve with the help of feedback from both my teacher and peers.

One of the most valuable lessons came from participating in peer reviews. Reviewing my classmates’ work was both insightful and challenging. On one hand, it helped me understand different approaches to writing, but on the other hand, assigning points felt stressful because I knew how difficult it is to gather and express thoughts as a student.

The outline exercise was another turning point for me. Before this class, I had never used an outline to start a paper. Learning how to organize my ideas into an outline made the writing process more manageable. I was able to translate my rough ideas into a draft and then improve it further. Through this process, I learned how to use transitions to connect sentences smoothly, how to write a proper introduction, and how to end with a strong conclusion.

As the semester went on, my confidence in writing grew. It didn’t necessarily become easier, but the structured approach we practiced helped guide me in the right direction. The skills I have gained from this class will benefit me in my future courses and even in my career.

Thank you, Mrs.castaldo, for teaching me the fundamentals of writing that I can carry forward in both school and work.
